Celestial bodies

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in our Solar System and closest to the Sun. It has a rocky surface and extreme temperature changes.

Orbital Period: 88 days

Distance from Sun: 57,900,000 km

Mass: 3.30 × 10^23 kg

Diameter: 4,879 km

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is often called Earth's "sister planet" due to their similar size and mass. It has a thick, toxic atmosphere and is the hottest planet in our Solar System.

Orbital Period: 224.7 days

Distance from Sun: 108,200,000 km

Mass: 4.87 × 10^24 kg

Diameter: 12,104 km

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet to support life. It has a diverse climate and a protective atmosphere that allows for a wide range of ecosystems.

Orbital Period: 365.25 days

Distance from Sun: 149,600,000 km

Mass: 5.97 × 10^24 kg

Diameter: 12,742 km

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is often referred to as the "Red Planet" due to its reddish appearance. It has a thin atmosphere and evidence of ancient riverbeds and lakes.

Orbital Period: 687 days

Distance from Sun: 227,900,000 km

Mass: 6.42 × 10^23 kg

Diameter: 6,779 km

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our Solar System and is a gas giant. It has a prominent Great Red Spot and many moons.

Orbital Period: 4,333 days

Distance from Sun: 778,500,000 km

Mass: 1.90 × 10^27 kg

Diameter: 139,820 km

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and is known for its prominent ring system. It is a gas giant with a composition similar to Jupiter.

Orbital Period: 10,759 days

Distance from Sun: 1,433,500,000 km

Mass: 5.68 × 10^26 kg

Diameter: 116,460 km

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and is an ice giant. It has a unique blue-green color due to methane in its atmosphere and rotates on its side.

Orbital Period: 30,687 days

Distance from Sun: 2,870,990,000 km

Mass: 8.68 × 10^25 kg

Diameter: 50,724 km

Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun. It is an ice giant with a deep blue color and has strong winds and storms.

Orbital Period: 10,759 days

Distance from Sun: 1,433,500,000 km

Mass: 1.02 × 10^26 kg

Diameter: 49,244 km

Pluto

Pluto is a dwarf planet in the outer Solar System. It has a highly elliptical and tilted orbit, and is composed primarily of ice and rock.

Orbital Period: 90,530 days

Distance from Sun: 5,906,400,000 km

Mass: 1.31 × 10^22 kg

Diameter: 2,370 km
